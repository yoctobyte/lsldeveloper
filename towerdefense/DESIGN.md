# Tower Defense: Sim-Wide Edition (2026)

## Overview
A high-fidelity Tower Defense experience designed for Second Life, capable of scaling from a tabletop board to a 256x256m sandbox simulation. The game features a multi-resource economy, autonomous wave management, and a HUD-less, dialog-driven interface.

---

## Core Game Mechanics

### 1. The Triad Economy
The game moves beyond simple "Gold" management by requiring the player to balance three distinct resources:
*   **Funds**: Used for purchasing and upgrading towers. Generated over time by the game engine.
*   **Energy (The Battery)**: Used for active tower operation and construction surges. 
    *   Stored in a **500-unit Backup Battery**.
    *   Generated by **Energy Plants** (Solar, Coal, or Nuclear).
    *   Consumed by every shot fired and every upgrade initiated.
*   **Karma**: A specialized resource consumed by offensive actions.
    *   Every projectile shot drains Karma.
    *   Requires the player to value every shot and avoid "spray and pray" tactics.

### 2. Sim-Wide Scaling
Using **LSD-Sequenced Self-Transport**, the game can be deployed across an entire region:
*   The **Game Engine** rezzes objects locally (within 10m).
*   Rezzed objects (Walkers/Towers) use **Linkset Data (LSD)** to retrieve their target region coordinates.
*   Objects use `llSetRegionPos` to instantly transport to their assigned grid square, bypassing the legacy 10m `llRezObject` limit.

### 3. Automated Wave Management
*   **Auto-Proceed**: The engine automatically sequences through levels defined in the `TD_GAME_LEVELS` notecard.
*   **Accounting**: The engine tracks every walker. Only when the entire wave is destroyed or has arrived does the next level countdown begin.
*   **Game Over**: If the player's Lives reach zero, the simulation stops immediately.

## Building Types

### Energy Plants
*   **Level 1 (Solar)**: Low, steady energy output.
*   **Level 2 (Coal)**: Medium output, higher construction drain.
*   **Level 3 (Nuclear)**: High output, requires significant battery capacity to manage.

### Defensive Towers
*   **Regenerative**: These towers focus on restoring player **Lives** or **Shields** over time, consuming Energy in the process.

### Attack Towers
*   **Ballistic**: Standard rapid-fire. Consumes 1 Energy and 1 Karma per shot.
*   **Sniper**: High damage, ignores shields. Consumes 5 Energy per shot.
*   **Fog/Toxic**: Area-of-effect towers that slow or damage multiple walkers simultaneously. Consumes constant Energy while active.

---

## Interface & Interaction
*   **NO HUD**: To maintain immersion, all interactions are handled via clicking the in-world objects.
*   **Dynamic Dialogs**: Context-sensitive menus allow for upgrading, selling, or checking status.
*   **Local Status**: The Engine and objects print tactical status updates to local chat, keeping the player informed of resource levels and wave progress.
